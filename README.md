# Analyze the Land Use Land Cover (LULC) Change in and Around Arial Beel through Remote Sensing and GIS

## Overview

This repository contains the research materials, workflow, datasets, analytical outputs, maps, and writing components of my Master’s thesis on **Land Use Land Cover (LULC) change in and around Arial Beel, Bangladesh**, using **Remote Sensing and GIS** techniques. The study examines temporal land use and land cover changes, wetland transformation, and the environmental implications of these changes in one of Bangladesh’s ecologically important wetland systems.

The repository is designed to document the complete research process in a structured way, from proposal development and literature review to geospatial analysis, result interpretation, map preparation, and thesis writing. It serves both as a thesis archive and as an academic research portfolio demonstrating the application of geospatial tools in environmental and wetland studies.

---

## Research Background

Wetlands are among the most productive and environmentally significant ecosystems, providing important ecological, hydrological, and socio-economic functions. Arial Beel is one of the notable wetland systems in Bangladesh, supporting biodiversity, agriculture, fisheries, water retention, and local livelihoods. However, changing land use practices, agricultural expansion, settlement growth, and other human interventions may alter the wetland environment over time.

Understanding the pattern and magnitude of land use and land cover change in and around Arial Beel is essential for wetland conservation, environmental planning, and sustainable land management. Remote Sensing and GIS provide effective tools for monitoring these changes over time and producing evidence-based insights for future decision-making.

---

## Research Objectives

The major objectives of this study are:

* To identify the major land use and land cover classes in and around Arial Beel.
* To analyze temporal changes in land use and land cover over selected years.
* To quantify the spatial extent of LULC changes using geospatial techniques.
* To examine wetland transformation patterns and land conversion processes.
* To assess the possible environmental implications of LULC change in the study area.
* To contribute to sustainable wetland management and environmental planning through spatial analysis.

---

## Study Area

The study focuses on **Arial Beel**, a wetland area in Bangladesh, and its surrounding landscape. The area is environmentally important for wetland ecology, agriculture, fisheries, water resources, and local livelihoods. The research investigates how land use patterns within and around this wetland have changed over time and what those changes may indicate for environmental sustainability.

---

## Research Questions

This study is guided by the following research questions:

1. What are the major land use and land cover categories in and around Arial Beel?
2. How have land use and land cover patterns changed over the selected time periods?
3. Which land cover classes have experienced the most significant increase or decrease?
4. What are the likely environmental implications of wetland and surrounding land cover transformation?
5. How can geospatial analysis support wetland conservation and sustainable land use planning in the study area?

## Methodological Framework

The thesis follows a geospatial workflow for analyzing land use and land cover change. The general methodological steps include:

1. **Study area selection and boundary preparation**
2. **Collection of satellite imagery and supporting spatial data**
3. **Image preprocessing and clipping to the study area**
4. **Selection of land use/land cover classes**
5. **Image classification using suitable geospatial techniques**
6. **Accuracy assessment of classified outputs**
7. **Area calculation for each LULC category**
8. **Temporal comparison and change detection analysis**
9. **Preparation of maps, tables, and visual outputs**
10. **Interpretation of environmental implications and discussion of findings**
