# Analyze the Land Use Land Cover (LULC) Change in and Around Arial Beel through Remote Sensing and GIS


#### `Abstract/` 
This study investigates Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) change in and around Arial Beel, Bangladesh, using Remote Sensing (RS) and Geographic Information System (GIS) techniques. Wetlands such as Arial Beel play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance, supporting biodiversity, regulating hydrological systems, and sustaining local livelihoods. However, rapid land use transformation driven by agricultural expansion, settlement growth, and human interventions has significantly altered wetland landscapes over time.

Multi-temporal satellite imagery (Landsat and/or Sentinel) was used to analyze LULC changes over selected time periods. Supervised classification techniques were applied to categorize land cover into major classes such as water bodies, wetlands, agricultural land, vegetation, built-up areas, and bare land. GIS tools were used to quantify spatial and temporal changes, while change detection analysis helped identify land conversion patterns.

The results indicate a noticeable reduction in wetland and water body areas, accompanied by a significant increase in agricultural and settlement land. These changes reflect strong anthropogenic pressure on the wetland ecosystem. The study also highlights spatial variation in land transformation, with more rapid changes occurring in accessible and peripheral zones.

The findings demonstrate the effectiveness of Remote Sensing and GIS in monitoring environmental change and provide valuable insights for sustainable land management and wetland conservation in Bangladesh.

#### `02_Literature_Review/`

2.1 Introduction

Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) change is a widely studied topic in environmental science, geography, and remote sensing due to its direct link with environmental sustainability, ecosystem dynamics, and human–environment interactions. LULC change analysis helps to understand how natural landscapes are transformed by human activities such as agriculture expansion, urbanization, infrastructure development, and resource exploitation.

Remote Sensing (RS) and Geographic Information Systems (GIS) have become essential tools for monitoring and analyzing these changes over time. These technologies allow researchers to detect spatial and temporal variations in land cover with high accuracy and efficiency.

2.2 Concept of Land Use and Land Cover (LULC)

Land cover refers to the physical material on the earth’s surface such as water, vegetation, soil, and built-up areas. Land use refers to the human activities and purposes associated with these land covers, such as agriculture, settlement, industry, and conservation.

Understanding both land use and land cover is important because changes in one often influence the other, especially in environmentally sensitive areas such as wetlands.

2.3 Remote Sensing and GIS in LULC Studies

Remote sensing provides satellite-based data that enables large-scale environmental monitoring over time. GIS complements this by allowing spatial analysis, mapping, and visualization of land cover changes.

Common satellite data used in LULC studies include:

Landsat series (30m resolution, long-term historical data)
Sentinel-2 (10–20m resolution, high spatial detail)

Common techniques include:

Supervised classification (Maximum Likelihood, Random Forest)
Unsupervised classification
Change detection analysis
Post-classification comparison

These methods are widely used due to their ability to produce reliable land cover maps and quantify changes over time.

2.4 Wetlands and Their Importance

Wetlands are among the most productive ecosystems on Earth, providing ecological, hydrological, and socio-economic services. In Bangladesh, wetlands such as beels, haors, and baors play a crucial role in fisheries, agriculture, flood regulation, biodiversity conservation, and livelihood support.

However, wetlands are highly vulnerable to land use changes caused by:

Agricultural expansion
Settlement growth
Infrastructure development
Climate variability
Water management interventions
2.5 LULC Change in Wetland Regions of Bangladesh

Several studies in Bangladesh have shown significant wetland degradation and land conversion over recent decades. Research indicates that many wetlands are shrinking due to increasing human pressure, particularly agricultural intensification and urban expansion.

Key findings from previous studies include:

Reduction in water bodies and seasonal wetlands
Expansion of agricultural land in wetland zones
Increase in settlement areas near floodplain regions
Alteration of natural hydrological systems

These changes have led to ecological imbalance, biodiversity loss, and increased vulnerability to flooding and drought.

2.6 Application of RS and GIS in Wetland Studies

Remote sensing and GIS have been extensively used in wetland studies globally and in Bangladesh to:

Monitor wetland shrinkage and expansion
Detect seasonal water dynamics
Map land cover transitions
Assess environmental impacts of land conversion
Support wetland management and conservation planning

Studies have demonstrated that multi-temporal satellite analysis is highly effective in identifying long-term changes in wetland ecosystems.

2.7 Research Gap

Although several studies have focused on general wetland change in Bangladesh, limited detailed research has been conducted specifically on Arial Beel using recent high-resolution multi-temporal satellite data.

Key gaps include:

Lack of updated LULC maps for Arial Beel
Limited quantitative analysis of land conversion patterns
Insufficient integration of GIS-based spatial analysis and change detection
Limited assessment of environmental implications at local scale

This study aims to address these gaps by providing a detailed spatial and temporal analysis of LULC change in and around Arial Beel.

2.8 Summary

The literature indicates that LULC change is a major environmental concern in wetland regions, particularly in Bangladesh. Remote Sensing and GIS provide powerful tools for monitoring these changes over time. However, site-specific studies focusing on Arial Beel remain limited, highlighting the need for detailed spatial analysis, which this research aims to fulfill.

This section outlines the structure, development process, and organization of the Master’s thesis titled “Land Use Land Cover (LULC) Change in and Around Arial Beel using Remote Sensing and GIS.” The thesis is prepared through systematic academic writing based on geospatial analysis, literature review, and interpretation of multi-temporal satellite data.

The writing process follows a structured academic format to ensure clarity, consistency, and scientific rigor.

8.2 Thesis Structure

The thesis is organized into five major chapters:

Chapter 1: Introduction

This chapter includes the background of the study, problem statement, research objectives, research questions, scope, and significance of the study.

Chapter 2: Literature Review

This chapter presents existing research on LULC change, wetland dynamics, Remote Sensing applications, and GIS-based environmental studies, identifying research gaps in the study area.

Chapter 3: Data and Materials

This chapter describes satellite imagery, spatial datasets, study area boundary, classification scheme, and data preprocessing methods.

Chapter 4: Methodology

This chapter explains the research design, image preprocessing, classification techniques, accuracy assessment, and change detection methods.

Chapter 5: Results and Discussion

This chapter presents LULC maps, statistical analysis, temporal changes, spatial patterns, and environmental interpretation of results.

Chapter 6 (if included): Maps and Figures

This section provides visual representation of study area maps, classified LULC maps, and change detection outputs.

Chapter 7 (optional depending on format): Conclusion and Recommendations

This chapter summarizes findings and provides recommendations for sustainable land use and wetland management.

8.3 Writing Process

The thesis writing process is carried out in several stages:

Stage 1: Draft Preparation

Initial drafts of each chapter are written based on collected data, literature, and GIS analysis outputs.

Stage 2: Revision and Editing

Each chapter is reviewed and revised to improve clarity, academic tone, and logical flow.

Stage 3: Integration of Results

Maps, tables, graphs, and figures are integrated into relevant chapters to support analysis.

Stage 4: Formatting

The thesis is formatted according to university guidelines, including:

Font style and size
Heading structure
Figure and table numbering
Citation style (APA/Harvard/other)
Stage 5: Final Review

The final document is checked for consistency, accuracy, and completeness before submission.

The following tools are used during thesis preparation:

All academic sources used in the thesis are properly cited following standard referencing style (APA/Harvard). References include:

Peer-reviewed journal articles

Proper citation ensures academic integrity and avoids plagiarism.

8.6 Integration of GIS Outputs into Thesis

GIS and Remote Sensing outputs are systematically integrated into the thesis:

LULC maps are included in Results chapter

8.7 Final Thesis Compilation

The final thesis is compiled as a single document containing all chapters, figures, tables, and references. It represents a complete academic study of land use and land cover change in the study area.

The final output is used for:

Academic submission
Viva presentation

The thesis writing process follows a structured academic approach combining literature, geospatial analysis, and scientific writing. The integration of Remote Sensing and GIS outputs ensures that the study provides a comprehensive understanding of LULC change dynamics in Arial Beel, Bangladesh.

#### `11_Conclusion and Recommendations/` 



11.1 Conclusion

This study successfully analyzed Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) changes in and around Arial Beel using Remote Sensing and GIS techniques. The results clearly indicate significant transformation of the landscape over time, particularly the conversion of wetlands and water bodies into agricultural land and settlements.

The analysis shows that anthropogenic activities are the dominant driver of land cover change in the study area. Agricultural expansion, population pressure, and infrastructure development have contributed to continuous wetland degradation. Spatial analysis further reveals that land use change is more intense in accessible and peripheral regions, while core wetland areas show comparatively slower change.

Overall, the study confirms that Arial Beel has experienced substantial environmental change, which may have long-term implications for biodiversity, hydrology, and ecosystem sustainability.

11.2 Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following recommendations are proposed:

Strengthen wetland conservation policies and enforcement mechanisms to prevent further degradation.
Promote sustainable land use planning in and around wetland areas.
Implement continuous monitoring of wetlands using Remote Sensing and GIS technologies.
Encourage community awareness programs about the ecological importance of wetlands.
Control unplanned agricultural expansion and settlement growth in sensitive wetland zones.
Integrate scientific geospatial analysis into national and regional environmental planning.

11.3 Future Research Scope

Future studies can improve upon this research by:

Using higher-resolution satellite data for more precise classification
Applying machine learning algorithms for improved accuracy
Conducting field-based validation for ground truth verification
Expanding the study to seasonal and climate-based variations
Integrating socio-economic data with spatial analysis
11.4 Final Statement

This research highlights the importance of Remote Sensing and GIS as powerful tools for monitoring environmental change. The study provides a scientific basis for understanding land transformation processes in Arial Beel and supports sustainable environmental management strategies in Bangladesh.
